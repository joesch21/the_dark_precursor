## 1. Purpose and Scope

This investigation examines methods of concept mapping derived from or inspired by Gilles Deleuze and Félix Guattari (primarily *A Thousand Plateaus*, 1987), with attention to Ian Buchanan’s applied contributions. The goal is to identify approaches that can inform — without supplanting — the Buchanan Deleuze Intelligence Platform’s semantic concept layer and experimental psycho-linguistic modelling.

Deleuzian mapping is not a neutral representational technique. It is a productive, experimental practice oriented toward multiplicities, becomings, and lines of flight. It resists arborescent (tree-like, hierarchical, centered, genealogical) structures in favour of rhizomatic ones. The investigation therefore remains skeptical of any reductive translation into conventional graph databases or static taxonomies.

## 2. Philosophical Foundations: Rhizome and Cartography

From *A Thousand Plateaus* (Deleuze & Guattari, trans. Massumi, 1987), the rhizome is articulated through six principles, of which the final two — **cartography and decalcomania** — are most directly relevant to mapping:

- **Cartography (mapping)**: The map is open and connectable in all dimensions; detachable, reversible, susceptible to constant modification. It has multiple entryways and exits and its own lines of flight. It does not reproduce a prior structure but experiments with connections.
- **Decalcomania (tracing)**: The tracing reproduces existing structures, imposes centeredness, hierarchy, and genealogy. It is the “obverse” of the map — useful as a provisional slice but always to be laid back onto the map for further experimentation.

Key distinction (repeated across sources): **Make a map, not a tracing.** The map follows heterogeneous connections, asignifying ruptures, and lines of flight (vectors of escape, deterritorialization, creative transformation). It embraces multiplicity rather than unity or fixed identity.

Buchanan’s work (e.g., on assemblage theory and method, differential method, schizoanalysis) consistently treats concepts as tools for practical mapping onto social, cultural, and political assemblages — not as static definitions but as mobile operators that reveal movements, intensities, and transformations. His approach aligns with schizoanalytic cartographies (Guattari) that are diagrammatic and meta-modeling rather than representational.

## 3. Scholarly Applications and Reconfigurations of Concept Mapping

A significant body of work reconfigures traditional concept mapping (e.g., Novakian hierarchical propositional maps) through Deleuzian lenses, particularly in education and knowledge representation research:

- **Rhizomatic Concept Mapping (Kinchin et al., 2020, “Concept Mapping in the Age of Deleuze”)**:  
  Traditional maps are often “tracings” — linear, hierarchical, scored for accuracy, oriented toward fixed structures and status-quo knowledge building. Rhizomatic maps treat concept maps as provisional slices through complex multiplicities. They emphasize:
  - Process over product (becoming rather than being).
  - Horizontal, messy, non-centered networks with multiple connections and interpretations.
  - Lines of flight and assemblages as sites of creative potential and disruption.
  - Serial/progressive mapping over time, with maps laid back onto developing understanding through reflection and feedback.
  - Rejection of arbitrary endpoints or reductive scoring in favour of qualitative morphology and emergent properties.

  Practical moves include using rhizomatic terminology in map construction, overlaying tracings on maps experimentally, and analyzing contestations or lines of articulation/flight within any organization.

- **Visualising Rhizomatic Learning with Cartography (Kairienė, 2024)**: Applies cartography and rhizoanalysis to student learning paths, depicting journeys via concepts such as nomads, lines of flight, and becoming. Maps are created as experimental visualizations rather than fixed representations.

- **Broader Themes**: Representation vs. cartography (tracing reproduces; mapping/diagramming/meta-modeling experiments). Guattari’s schizoanalytic cartographies extend this into diagrammatic, a-signifying registers. Buchanan’s assemblage method provides a concrete toolkit for mapping concepts onto real-world heterogeneous assemblages without reducing them to essences or hierarchies.

These approaches share a commitment to non-reductive fidelity: mapping discloses potential organizations and movements rather than reproducing prior ones. They are particularly attuned to zones of conceptual intensity, recursion, drift, and transformation — precisely the terrain of psycho-linguistic observation.

## 4. Relation to Platform Layers and the Vault

**Semantic Concept Layer**:  
The Vault’s Cartography API and concept-graph functionality already gesture toward dynamic mapping. Deleuzian methods suggest evolving this toward explicitly rhizomatic structures: heterogeneous relations, multi-directional connectivity, support for modifiable/overlays, lines of flight (perhaps modelled as rupture or drift edges), and multiplicity-preserving representations. Static taxonomies or strict ontologies would constitute tracings; the platform should privilege experimental, reversible graphs.

**Psycho-Linguistic Modelling (Experimental)**:  
Metaphor density (Level 2 embedding deviation and higher) can feed rhizomatic maps by surfacing passages or zones with high connective/transformative potential. Mapping then becomes a tool for observing how meaning is produced through conceptual movement, metaphorical blurring, and affective pressure — without claiming to model authorial intention. Outputs must remain labelled experimental and traceable to specific passages.

**The Dark Precursor Interface**:  
The Vault’s deployed cartography offers a reference pattern. Native implementation could support interactive, modifiable maps that allow operators to trace, overlay, follow lines of flight, and experiment with assemblages. This aligns with the interface’s cinematic/generative ethos and the goal of making observable the texture and force of philosophical language.

**Buchanan’s Contribution**:  
His emphasis on assemblage as method, differential analysis, and applied schizoanalysis provides a bridge between philosophical concepts and concrete mapping practices. The scholars.json and groups.json already persisted from the Vault supply a starting cluster (Deleuzian Scholars with default concepts) that can be mapped rhizomatically rather than hierarchically.

## 5. Risks, Limitations, and Safeguards

- **Reduction to Conventional Graphs**: Rhizomatic mapping is not merely “network analysis” or “knowledge graphs.” It resists centeredness, hierarchy, and closure. Any technical implementation must preserve experimental openness (multiple entry points, reversibility, lines of flight) or risk becoming a sophisticated tracing.
- **Over-Interpretation**: Maps disclose movements and potentials; they do not interpret or attribute positions to Buchanan/Deleuze & Guattari unless supported by stronger citation evidence.
- **Scope Creep**: This investigation is reference only. No changes to core tables, Evidence Cards, or The Dark Precursor have been made. Integration requires patch bundle and human review.
- **Evidence Spine**: All mapping observations must remain read-only with respect to canonical sources/passages/citations. Psycho-linguistic outputs stay secondary.
- **Reproducibility & Governance**: Baseline methods (e.g., embedding deviation for density) and any graph algorithms must be documented; human review gate before surface influence.

## 7. Conceptual Fidelity

Deleuzian concept mapping methods do not offer a technique for mastering or fixing meaning. They offer instruments for following how concepts move, connect heterogeneously, rupture, and transform — how they participate in the production of new intensities and assemblages. In the context of the Platform, they enlarge the capacity to observe the texture, recursion, and force through which philosophical language works, while insisting that such observation remains experimental, human-governed, and always laid back onto the map for further becoming.

No claim is advanced that these methods deliver objective or complete representations. They are tools for attentive experimentation with the very movements the psycho-linguistic layer seeks to make inspectable.
