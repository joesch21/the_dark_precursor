## Purpose

This document catalogues the key passages from Ian Buchanan’s *Assemblage Theory and Method* (2021) that have been reviewed during BDP-002F. These passages are selected for their value in articulating Buchanan’s distinctive reading of assemblage theory.

All excerpts are short, rights-aware, and recorded for later governed citation phases only.

## Reviewed Passages

### Category 1: Core Definition & Method

**Passage 1.1**  
**Page:** 2–4  
**Theme:** Critique of existing assemblage theory and call to return to Deleuze & Guattari  
**Short Excerpt:**  
> "Assemblage has all but become a ‘received idea’... Any and every ‘thing’, or more precisely, any and every kind of collection of things has in recent times been called an assemblage."

**Status:** Reviewed  
**Notes:** Strong opening statement on the dilution of the concept.
