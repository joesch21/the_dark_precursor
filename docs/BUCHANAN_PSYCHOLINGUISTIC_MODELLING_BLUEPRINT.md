# Buchanan Psycho-Linguistic Modelling Blueprint

**Status:** Foundational Reference Document  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** June 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the governing principles, boundaries, and process for developing psycho-linguistic modelling capabilities within the Buchanan Deleuze Intelligence Platform.

The goal of psycho-linguistic modelling is to move beyond purely propositional retrieval and concept mapping toward an understanding of **how meaning is produced through the movement, texture, and force of philosophical language**.

This layer is explicitly secondary to the citation, provenance, and semantic concept layers. It must never override or contradict evidence-based records.

---

## 2. Vision

The platform ultimately aims to support research into **semantic embodiment** — the idea that in thinkers such as Deleuze, Guattari, and Buchanan, meaning is not only carried by explicit statements but is also enacted through:

- Metaphorical density and transformation
- Conceptual recursion and drift
- Rhythmic and tonal shifts
- Affective pressure and destabilisation
- Semantic elasticity and ambiguity

Psycho-linguistic modelling exists to make some of these dimensions observable and inspectable, while remaining under strict human governance.

---

## 3. Core Principles

### 3.1 Hierarchy of Authority
1. Citation and provenance evidence (primary)
2. Semantic concept records and relations
3. Psycho-linguistic modelling observations (secondary / experimental)
4. Reader transformation analysis (future / tertiary)

Psycho-linguistic observations must always be traceable back to specific passages and must carry clear authority labels.

### 3.2 Description Before Claim
Psycho-linguistic modelling may describe patterns, densities, movements, and tensions in the text.  
It must **not** attribute intentions, positions, or theoretical consequences to Buchanan (or Deleuze & Guattari) unless supported by stronger evidence layers.

### 3.3 Experimental Status
All psycho-linguistic modelling capabilities shall initially be developed as **experimental modules**. They must be:
- Isolated from core platform functionality
- Clearly labelled as experimental
- Subject to human review before any integration
- Reversible without damaging the evidence spine

### 3.4 Governance First
No psycho-linguistic dimension shall be implemented without:
- A defined modelling approach
- Clear output labelling rules
- Human review requirements
- Defined limitations and failure modes

## 4. Relationship to Existing Layers

| Layer | Role | Interaction with Psycho-Linguistic Modelling |
|-------|------|---------------------------------------------|
| Citation & Provenance | Foundation | All modelling must be passage-linked |
| Semantic Concept | Structural | Modelling can enrich but not redefine concepts |
| Psycho-Linguistic | Observational | Current focus of this blueprint |
| Reader Transformation | Future | May build on psycho-linguistic observations later |

Psycho-linguistic work must remain **read-only** with respect to canonical tables (`sources`, `passages`, `citations`, `concept_mentions`, etc.).

---

## 5. Process for Introducing New Modelling Dimensions

When proposing a new psycho-linguistic dimension (e.g., metaphor density, conceptual recursion, affective pressure), the following must be documented:

1. Clear definition of what is being modelled
2. Why it is relevant to Buchanan / Deleuze & Guattari studies
3. Proposed technical approach (with levels of sophistication)
4. Governance and review model
5. Known limitations and risks of misinterpretation
6. How outputs will be labelled and presented to the operator

Only after these elements are defined may implementation begin.

---

## 6. Output Labelling Requirements

All psycho-linguistic modelling outputs must include at minimum:

- **Authority Label**: e.g., `experimental_modelling`, `llm_assisted_human_reviewed`, `provisional_observation`
- **Evidence Link**: Specific passage(s) or concept(s) the observation is derived from
- **Limitations Statement**: What the model cannot reliably capture
- **Review Status**: Whether the output has been human-reviewed

---

## 7. Current Status (June 2026)

As of this document:
- The citation and semantic concept layers are under active development.
- Psycho-linguistic modelling remains in the **blueprint and early experimentation** stage.
- No psycho-linguistic observations are integrated into operator-facing surfaces (Evidence Cards, etc.).
- Metaphor density has been identified as the first modelling dimension to explore (see `BUCHANAN_METAPHOR_DENSITY_APPROACH.md`).

---

## 8. Success Criteria for This Layer

Psycho-linguistic modelling will be considered successful when it can:
- Reliably surface linguistically interesting passages for human review
- Provide observations that enrich (rather than replace) traditional close reading
- Maintain clear separation between descriptive modelling and interpretive claims
- Scale gracefully as more sources and passages are added to the platform
