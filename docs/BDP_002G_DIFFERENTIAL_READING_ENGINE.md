**Status:** Governed application contract

---

## 1. Core Thesis

The Buchanan / Deleuze Intelligence Platform is not merely a database of texts, citations, summaries, or concepts.

It is a **differential reading engine**.

The purpose of the platform is to help a reader move from:

> What does this concept mean?

to:

> How does this concept operate in a specific assemblage?

The platform must therefore support an analysis of **flows, cuts, capture, desire, affect, qualitative difference, and lines of flight**.

---

## 2. What the Platform Must Not Become

The platform must not become:

1. A flat citation warehouse.
2. A generic Deleuze explainer.
3. A metaphor generator.
4. A loose assemblage map where everything connects to everything.
5. A psycho-linguistic inference engine that outranks textual evidence.
6. A system that invents Buchanan-specific claims without citation and review.

The danger is analytic flattening: treating assemblage theory as additive connectivity.

The platform preserves the cutting edge of the method by asking what is interrupted, captured, extracted, intensified, or released.

---

## 3. Differential Reading Contract

Every differential analysis card must answer the following questions.

### 3.1 Assemblage

What is the specific assemblage being analysed?

Name the actual system, practice, institution, technical machine, textual machine, social scene, cultural form, or concept-machine.

Avoid vague causal containers such as "society", "capitalism", "media", "technology", or "psychology" unless the analysis specifies exactly how that force operates in the case.

### 3.2 Flow

What is flowing?

Possible flows include:

- desire
- affect
- attention
- images
- bodies
- labour
- money
- language
- signs
- matter
- data
- memory
- intensity
- social energy

The platform must ask: what is being organized, interrupted, diverted, accelerated, captured, extracted, or released?

### 3.3 Cut / Interruption / Subtraction

Where is the cut?

Assemblages do not merely connect. They also cut, select, filter, interrupt, redirect, subtract, block, release, and render continuous.

A key operational principle:

> Continuity is often produced by interruption or subtraction.

Therefore the analysis must identify:

- what discontinuity is removed
- what barrier is subtracted
- what threshold is created
- what is cut away
- what new continuity appears because of the cut

### 3.4 Capture / Extraction

What does the assemblage capture or extract?

Examples include:

- attention
- value
- obedience
- subjectivity
- visibility
- addiction
- social comparison
- surplus labour
- affective intensity
- data
- belief
- identity
- control

The platform must not treat assemblages as neutral networks.

### 3.5 Desire

What desire is being assembled?

Desire is not treated as a private psychological property. Desire is productive and assembling.

The correct question is not only:

> What does this person want?

The stronger question is:

> What assemblage is producing, organizing, directing, or capturing this desire?

### 3.6 Affect / Intensity

What affect or intensity is produced?

The analysis must identify:

- what intensities circulate
- what mood or charge is produced
- what capacity to act is increased or decreased
- what kind of reader, body, user, consumer, subject, or participant is formed

Affect is part of the operating system of the assemblage, not decoration added after the fact.

### 3.7 Qualitative Difference

What difference matters?

Do not classify things by material similarity alone.

The same object, sign, body, phrase, or technology can enter different assemblages and produce different effects.

Example: an axe can function as a tool or a weapon. The material object may be similar, but the assemblage, affect, speed, direction, and force differ.

### 3.8 Line of Flight

Where can the flow escape, mutate, or become otherwise?

The platform must not only describe capture. It must also preserve the possibility of mutation, experimentation, and becoming.

Ask:

- Where does the flow exceed capture?
- What does the assemblage fail to contain?
- What alternative becoming appears?
- What outside is opened?

## 4. Authority Boundary

Differential analysis is a method of reading. It is not automatic authority.

Every output must label its authority.

Allowed authority labels:

1. `primary_text_backed`
2. `buchanan_direct`
3. `secondary_scholarship`
4. `source_bound_description`
5. `system_synthesis`
6. `user_interpretation`
7. `experimental_modelling`

Blocked without later governed evidence:

- "Buchanan argues X"
- "Buchanan's position is X"
- "The meaning of Body without Organs in Buchanan is X"
- "This proves Buchanan intended X"
- Any author-position claim without exact passage, citation, and review

The evidence spine remains primary. Model inference never outranks citation.

## 5. Application Surfaces

This contract can be used in four places.

### 5.1 Concept Cards

Each major concept card can contain a "Differential Reading" section:

- assemblage
- flow
- cut
- capture
- desire
- affect
- qualitative difference
- line of flight
- authority label
- evidence chain

### 5.2 Evidence Cards

Evidence cards may add a non-authorial, clearly labelled section:

> Differential Reading Prompt

This section should ask the operator how the cited passage might support or block a later differential analysis.

It must not convert a cited passage into an interpretation without review.

### 5.3 Semantic Workbench

The semantic workbench should use this method to prevent flattening.

Instead of only showing related concepts, it should ask:

- What relation type is actually operating?
- What is cut or captured?
- What flow is being traced?
- What is the qualitative difference between adjacent concepts?

### 5.4 Dark Precursor Interface

The Dark Precursor can surface a "Differential Engine" panel that shows the template and invites the operator to test a case.

The panel must remain governed and labelled as methodological guidance until connected to reviewed evidence.

## 6. Minimum Viable Engine

The first usable version of the differential engine does not need a full database redesign.

Minimum viable engine:

1. Add this doctrine.
2. Add the LLM prompt contract.
3. Add the analysis card JSON schema.
4. Add a verifier.
5. Add optional frontend panel.
6. Test on one example, such as:
   - social media image-feed
   - axe as tool vs weapon
   - Body without Organs as subtraction of organismic capture

This is enough to make the system feel what it is for.

---

## 7. Success Standard

A successful platform answer should not merely say:

> The Body without Organs is related to desire, assemblage, and affect.

It should say, with proper authority labels:

> In this governed reading, the concept becomes operational when we identify the assemblage, the flow being organized, the cut or subtraction that produces continuity, the form of capture or release, the desire being assembled, the affective intensity produced, the qualitative difference that matters, and the possible line of flight.

---

## 8. One-Line Operator Rule

**Do not map everything. Find the cut. Follow the flow. Name the capture. Preserve the difference.**

---

## 9. Boundary Statement

BDP-002G is a doctrine and application-contract slice.

It does not:

- insert passages
- insert citations
- create concept relations
- create interpretations
- change the database schema
- implement hidden personalisation
- infer psychological states
- claim Buchanan-specific authority

It prepares the application to read differentially while keeping the evidence spine in command.
